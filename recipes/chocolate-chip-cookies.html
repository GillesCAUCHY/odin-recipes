Chocolate Chip Cookies Recipe

A Timeless Chocolate Chip Cookies Recipe

Chocolate chip cookies are a classic American treat loved for their crispy edges and soft, chewy centers. To begin, cream together softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in eggs one at a time, followed by vanilla extract, to create a smooth base. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt, then gradually mix these dry ingredients into the wet mixture. Finally, fold in chocolate chips to distribute them evenly throughout the dough.

Drop rounded spoonfuls of dough onto a lined baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading. Bake in a preheated oven at 375°F (190°C) for 9–11 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown but the centers are still soft. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for 2 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack. Serve warm with a glass of milk, or store in an airtight container for up to a week.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups chocolate chips

Steps to achieve our Chocolate Chip Cookies Recipe

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cream butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar together.
  3. Beat in eggs, one at a time, then add vanilla.
  4. Whisk flour, baking soda, and salt in a separate bowl.
  5. Gradually mix dry ingredients into the wet mixture.
  6. Fold in chocolate chips.
  7. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto a lined baking sheet.
  8. Bake for 9–11 minutes until edges are golden.
  9. Cool on the baking sheet for 2 minutes.
  10. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
